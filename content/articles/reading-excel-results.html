After completing a retirement simulation in DynaRetire, an Excel file with personalized results is generated. While the spreadsheet contains valuable information about financial futures, it might seem overwhelming at first. This guide provides assistance in understanding what this data means for retirement planning.

Excel Structure: A Retirement Story in Four Chapters

DynaRetire results are organized into four main sheets:

  • Summary Sheet: The "big picture" view showing year-by-year cash flow from now until retirement end
  • Expenses Sheet: A detailed spending plan across all categories over time
  • Cash-in Incomes Sheet: All money flowing into accounts (besides investments)
  • Retirement Funds Sheet: How money moves between accounts, grows, and gets used

Summary Sheet: Overall Picture

The Summary sheet tracks money's cash flow each year:

  1. Beginning Balance (BOY BAL): Starting funds
  2. Expenses (BOY Expenses): Money set aside for that year's costs
  3. Balance After Expenses: What remains to earn returns
  4. Investment Returns (P/L): What investments earned/lost
  5. Income: Additional money from sources like rental income
  6. Ending Balance (EOY BAL): Year-end total becoming next year's starting point

Key Insights from the Summary Sheet

  • The Money Flow Cycle: Follow how each year progresses from beginning balance → expenses → investment returns → income → ending balance
  • Simulation End Point: The simulation automatically stops when the ending balance becomes insufficient to cover the next year's expenses
  • Pre-Retirement Growth: Observe how the balance grows during working years
  • Major Expense Impact: Notice how one-time expenses (like car purchases) create spikes
  • Market Volatility: Identify years with dramatic market swings in the P/L column
  • Balance Trajectory: Follow the EOY BAL to see if money grows or shrinks over time
  • Longevity Indication: Check when the simulation ends to see if the money lasts the expected lifetime

Warning Signs to Watch For

  • Early End: Simulation stops before the planned maximum age
  • Consistently Declining Balance: EOY BAL decreases year after year
  • Expenses Exceed Income: Relying heavily on savings rather than ongoing income sources

Expenses Sheet: Where Money Goes

The Expenses sheet provides a detailed breakdown of spending over time:

Key Features to Understand

  • Year-by-Year Expense Calendar: Each row is a year, each column a spending category
  • Big-Ticket Items: One-time or periodic major purchases show as spikes
  • Inflation's Impact: Observe how expenses grow over time due to inflation
  • Minimum Spending Mode: During market downturns, reducible expenses appear in red, showing risk management strategies in action
  • Expense Evolution: Watch how spending categories shift in importance over time (e.g., travel early, healthcare later)

Using the Expenses Sheet Effectively

  • Check if spending priorities match the retirement vision
  • Identify years with expense spikes to ensure funds can manage them
  • Monitor inflation's impact, especially in categories with higher inflation rates
  • Evaluate if later-year expenses seem sustainable

Cash-in Incomes Sheet: Money Sources

This sheet maps money flowing into retirement accounts besides investment returns:

Key Elements to Understand

  • Income Timeline: Track how income sources change from working years to retirement
  • Increase Patterns: See how each income source grows over time
  • Deposit Destinations: Where each income source goes (which affects returns)
  • Income Stability: Evaluate how reliable various income streams are

Retirement Funds Sheet: Following Money's Journey

The most detailed sheet showing how money flows through each investment account:

Understanding Fund Activity

For each fund, the following details are provided:

  • Beginning Balance: Starting amount
  • Expenses: Withdrawals based on fund access rules
  • Remaining Balance: What's left after expenses
  • Return Rate: Percentage gained/lost that year
  • Profit/Loss: Actual dollar amount gained/lost
  • Deposits: New money coming in
  • Rebalancing: Money transferred between funds to maintain target allocations
  • Ending Balance: Final amount after all transactions

Critical Insights from the Funds Sheet

  • Fund Access Rules: See how withdrawal restrictions affect which funds cover expenses
  • Rebalancing Strategy: Watch how portfolios maintain target allocations over time
  • Return Differences: Compare how different fund types respond to market conditions
  • Risk Management: Observe protective strategies activating during market downturns

Risk Management Features in Action

Risk management strategies directly affect how retirement unfolds:

  • Minimum Spending Mode: Reduces discretionary expenses during poor market years
  • Rebalancing Strategies: Maintains target allocations and potentially pauses selling risky assets during downturns
  • Asset Allocation Shifts: Shows how portfolios become more conservative with age

Tips for Reading Results Effectively

  • Use Excel Features: Leverage frozen panes, cell comments, and color coding
  • Track Key Transitions: Pay special attention to milestone ages in the plan
  • Monitor Risk Effects: Look for how protective strategies activate during market downturns
  • Verify Cross-Sheet Flows: Confirm values match across different sheets
  • Focus on Trends: Look for patterns rather than fixating on specific numbers

Making the Most of DynaRetire Results

The DynaRetire Excel file is more than just numbers—it's a detailed blueprint of a financial future based on unique inputs. The data can be used to:

  • Identify potential risks decades before they create an impact
  • Evaluate how risk management strategies perform in various market scenarios
  • Refine retirement plans by comparing different scenarios
  • Optimize spending strategy to get the most from retirement.
  • Build confidence in retirement decisions through transparent projections

This data transforms retirement planning from abstract concern to concrete action—a living document that can be revisited as circumstances evolve.
