DynaRetire Showcase: How Martin Simulates His Early Retirement

Learn how Martin uses DynaRetire to test his early retirement plans at age 50. This showcase walks through a real-world retirement simulation including dynamic expenses, investment strategies, and risk management.

The Brief Scenario

Martin is 45 and wants to plan for early retirement at age 50. His retirement planning includes:

  • Continuing pension fund contributions with employer matching
  • Additional retirement-specific investment savings
  • Rental income from an apartment
  • Dynamic living expenses with varying inflation rates
  • Planning for medical expenses in old age
  • Travel plans during early retirement years
  • Housing loan payments until age 60
  • Insurance upgrades and car replacements
  • Expense adjustments as he ages

Current Financial Position

As of 2024, Martin has:

  • $1,000,000 in pension funds (5.5% expected growth, accessible at 55)
  • $500,000 in S&P 500 investments
  • Annual rental income of $10,000

Dynamic Expense Planning

Travel Budget

$100,000 allocated for the first two years of retirement for global travel, followed by $10,000 annually until age 75. Expected inflation: 3% per year.

Housing Loan

$10,000 annual payments continuing until age 60.

Insurance

Currently $4,000 annually, with a planned upgrade at age 56 adding $3,000 per year, continuing until age 80.

Medical Expenses

Starting at age 70, allocating $20,000 annually (in today's value) for health-related expenses. Expected medical inflation: 8% per year.

Car Replacement

Plans to buy a new car every 7 years starting at age 51, costing $60,000 net of old car sale. Last purchase planned at age 72. Expected inflation: 2% per year.

Food and Dining

Current budget of $20,000 annually, reducing to $15,000 by age 60. Expected food inflation: 6% per year. Can reduce to 80% during market downturns.

Transportation

$4,000 annual budget, reducing to $3,000 after age 65, and eliminating at age 80. Expected inflation: 4% per year.

Miscellaneous Expenses

$20,000 annually for bills, clothes, gadgets, repairs, etc. Expected inflation: 3.5% per year.

Investment Strategy and Risk Management

Martin's investment strategy includes:

  • Asset allocation shifts:
    • 70% stocks/30% bonds until age 60
    • 60% stocks/40% bonds from 60-65
    • 50% stocks/50% bonds after 65
  • Annual portfolio rebalancing
  • Minimum spending mode activation if average returns fall below 3%
  • Avoiding selling risky investments during market downturns

How The App Works

There are four steps to consider when simulating your retirement scenario. The first and second steps are essential, while the third and fourth steps are optional. You can jump to any step without following the sequence.

Results Analysis

The retirement simulation generates a comprehensive Excel file with four key sections:

  1. Summary: Overall fund balance, expenses, income, and P&L
  2. Expenses: Detailed breakdown of all expenses year by year
  3. Income: Comprehensive income source tracking
  4. Funds Flow: Cash flow tracking between different investment accounts

Conclusion

DynaRetire enables thorough retirement planning by accounting for:

  • Dynamic expense changes throughout retirement
  • Multiple income streams and investment strategies
  • Risk management and market condition responses
  • Long-term financial projections with detailed reporting
