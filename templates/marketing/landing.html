Our Mission

NumberWalk was created to support the financial independence planning community, built by someone who understands the real challenges of thoughtfully balancing savings, spending, growth, and security on the path to financial freedom.

A Tool, Not a Replacement

While NumberWalk offers comprehensive simulations, we recognize the value of professional financial advisors who provide personalized guidance, tax expertise, and emotional support during financial decisions. This tool complements, rather than replaces, professional advice.

Community-Minded

We believe financial planning tools should be accessible to everyone. NumberWalk exists to help people understand the dynamics of financial independence planning through interactive modeling, regardless of their wealth level or financial literacy.

The simulations provided are educational tools to help with planning decisions. For comprehensive financial advice tailored to your specific situation, we encourage consulting with a qualified financial professional.

Why Traditional Tools Aren't Enough

Old Way

  • Assumes constant withdrawal throughout retirement
  • Uses single inflation rate for all expenses
  • Requires manual calculation of retirement savings
  • Oversimplified investment assumptions with flat return
  • Missing effective risk handling as part of the plan
  • Must re-enter data or save data on the server
  • Missing important consideration for FIRE planning

NumberWalk Way

  • Adapts to changing expenses at different life stages
  • Different inflation rates for different expense types
  • Help you to project with contributions, returns, & growth
  • Real history market testing*
  • Switch to Minimum Spending for reducible expenses
  • Save/load plans locally‚Äîadjust easily with full privacy
  • Cater for pension fund withdrawal constraints

* Why We Use Historical Testing Instead of Monte Carlo

Market crashes are a historical pattern. When one hits early in retirement, your financial plans may suffer. Unlike random simulations, historical testing can provide insight and simulate how they could impact your retirement cash flow, so you can have a plan B or make adjustments if needed.
